# QA Bot Phone Script Training - Key Notes

## 4 Objectives of an Information Phone Call
1. Obtain Pertinent Information: Name, Phone Number, Email
2. Connect Them with Their 'WHY': Discover their motivation for calling
3. Guide the Conversation and Reflect Their WHY Back to Them
4. Book the Appointment: Secure a day and time for their visit

## Proper Etiquette
- Smile when you answer the phone
- Tailor the conversation to each person's wants and needs
- Avoid sounding too scripted; be genuine and natural
- Answer every phone call by the second ring
- Use proper grammar and professionalism
- Work to develop rapport and make the caller feel valued
- If unsure: 'I would be happy to get the answer for you. Let me get your name and number, and I'll get back to you shortly.'

## INBOUND CALL - 13 Steps

### 1. Greeting
- "It's a GREAT day at [School Name], may I ask who I am speaking with?"
- Follow-up: "Hi [First Name], what's your last name?"

### 2. Identify the Caller
- "Are you calling for yourself or your child?"
- If for a child: "May I ask the child's age?"

### 3. Confirm Location
- "Are you interested in our [Street] location?"

### 4. Determine Lead Source
- "May I ask how you heard about us?"

### 5. Identify Desired Benefits
- "May I ask what benefits you hope you or your child will gain by training with us?"
- Adults: Fitness, Get-In-Shape, Self-Defense, Community, Hobby, Competition/Training
- Kids: Discipline, Focus, Confidence, Fitness, Self-Defense
- If they say "To learn martial arts" → probe deeper: "What specific benefits are you hoping to gain?"

### 6. Current Efforts
- "May I ask if you're currently doing anything to achieve these benefits?"
- Adults: Ask about previous training or gym memberships
- Parents: Inquire about previous sports or professional recommendations

### 7. Position Your School
- Explain why your school is the best fit
- Address their desired benefits
- Share your Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
- Introduce your program genuinely and enthusiastically

### 8. Present the Offer
- "Have you heard about our [Offer]?"
- Explain the offer and include bonuses or freebies
- Transition smoothly to booking: "Which works better for you, tomorrow afternoon or evening?"

### 9. Schedule the Appointment
- Offer specific time slots: "I have availability tonight at 6 or tomorrow at 7—what works better?"
- Book appointments as soon as possible (same day or next)

### 10. Gather Information
- Collect: Full Name, Email, Phone, Age of potential student
- Verify accuracy: Repeat details back using phonetic clarification (e.g., 'P as in Peter')
- For failed appointments: Offer a free video resource and collect details for follow-up

### 11. Ask for Referrals
- "Do you have any friends who may also be interested?"
- If yes, collect names and numbers
- Offer to coordinate a private lesson or group session

### 12. Pre-Frame the Sign-Up
- "When you visit, if you like what you see, we can get you signed up—fair enough?"

### 13. Provide Next Steps
- "Make sure to wear workout clothes and bring your ID. We'll see you on [Day/Time]."
- Include parking tips or other relevant information

## General Notes / Key Techniques

### 1. Build the RELATIONSHIP
- Business in its most simplistic form is about relationships

### 2. Redirection
- Answer their question quickly and continue with script

### 3. "What is it about Karate that you like?"
- If they mention a martial art or service you don't provide, find out what they seek from it and mention you offer the same benefits

### 4. Pricing Questions
- DON'T say "I'm not allowed to give prices on the phone" — that raises suspicion
- Instead, go straight into the WHY and say:
  "At [School Name], we have programs that range from $X per month to $Y per month depending on what you are interested in taking. What we normally do is have you come in for your free private, then afterwards have you sit down with the program director so that he can determine which program is best for you…"
- Immediately after, ask another question and resume control of the conversation
